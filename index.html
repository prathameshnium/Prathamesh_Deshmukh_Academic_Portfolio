I am a PhD researcher at the UGC-DAE Consortium for Scientific Research, Mumbai, working on multiferroic composites and magnetoelectric coupling. My research bridges experimental material synthesis with computational methods, and I develop open-source Python tools for precise instrument control and data acquisition.

Research Profile

Exploring the intersection of magnetism, electricity, and crystal structure in novel materials.

Core Focus: Multiferroic Composites

My work centers on fabricating and characterizing materials with intrinsic magnetoelectric coupling. Key interests span next-generation sensors, non-volatile memory devices, and the fundamental study of spin–charge–lattice correlations.

Key Activities

  • Novel material synthesis (e.g., BaFe10Sc2O19 : KNbO3)
  • Design of custom probes for cryogenic measurements
  • Structural analysis via neutron & X-ray diffraction
  • Full magnetic and dielectric property characterization
